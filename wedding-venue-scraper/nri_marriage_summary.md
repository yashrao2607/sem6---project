# Summary: Marriages to Overseas Indians Booklet (MEA India)

This document provides an in-depth summary of the official booklet issued by the **Ministry of External Affairs (MEA), India**, regarding the legalities, risks, and safety measures for marriages involving Non-Resident Indians (NRIs).

---

## 1. Core Legal Requirements
For a marriage to an NRI to be legally recognized and protective, the following steps are mandatory:

*   **Compulsory Registration:** It is mandatory to register the marriage under the *Hindu Marriage Act 1955*, *Special Marriage Act 1954*, or *Foreign Marriage Act 1969* before leaving India. This creates a legal record that is recognized globally.
*   **Single Status Certificate:** The NRI spouse must provide an **Apostilled Single Status Affidavit** (Certificate of No Impediment) from their country of residence. This is the only legal proof that they are not already married abroad.
*   **Documentation Checklist:** 
    *   Original and photocopies of passports.
    *   Visa/PIO/OCI card copies.
    *   Proof of residence and employment in the foreign country.
    *   Recent tax returns (last 3 years).
*   **Social Validity:** The booklet recommends a publicized social ceremony to establish the marriage's existence in the community, preventing "secret" marriages.

## 2. Risk Factors & Common Scams
The MEA identifies several patterns of exploitation in NRI marriages:
*   **Abandonment:** The spouse is abandoned in India shortly after the wedding or left in a foreign land without financial or legal support.
*   **Fraudulent Status:** NRIs concealing existing marriages, domestic partners, or children in the foreign country.
*   **Ex-parte Divorce:** Obtaining a divorce in a foreign court without serving notice to the Indian spouse, often to escape alimony or legal responsibilities.
*   **Dowry & Harassment:** Using the spouse's isolation abroad to demand additional money or property.

## 3. Essential Precautions ("The Verification Protocol")
*   **Independent Background Check:** Never rely solely on marriage bureaus or middlemen. Verify employment and residence through local contacts or Indian Missions.
*   **Financial Safety:** The spouse should have a separate bank account in the foreign country and keep their original passport in their own possession at all times.
*   **Legal Awareness:** Research the host country's laws on domestic violence, divorce, and child custody before moving.
*   **Emergency Contacts:** Keep numbers for the local Indian Embassy and the NCW NRI Cell handy.

## 4. Government Support Ecosystem
*   **MEA Madad Portal:** A digital platform for filing and tracking grievances related to consular services and matrimonial disputes.
*   **National Commission for Women (NCW):** Operates a dedicated **NRI Cell** to provide legal aid, counseling, and assistance in tracking runaway grooms.
*   **Indian Missions Abroad:** The first point of contact for Indians in distress, providing temporary shelter and legal referrals.
*   **Indian Community Welfare Fund (ICWF):** Provides financial assistance for legal fees and return airfare for distressed Indians.
